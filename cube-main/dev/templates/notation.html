Notation

Notations for Rubik's Cube algorithms serve as a standardized language to describe the movements needed to solve the puzzle. Each notation represents a specific rotation of one of the cube's six faces. For example, "R" denotes a clockwise rotation of the right face, while "R'" indicates a counterclockwise rotation. This standardized system allows cubers worldwide to share and learn algorithms efficiently, ensuring consistent understanding and execution. The notations are essential for documenting and teaching the sequences required to solve different parts of the cube, from simple maneuvers to complex solving strategies

Moves with " ' " like R', U' are the reverse of the normal moves
Moves in lower case like r, u are wide moves where you move two layers
